# Social Engineering

## Overview

Social engineering is a manipulation technique that exploits human psychology to gain access to sensitive information or systems.

Instead of attacking systems directly, attackers target people.

---

## How It Works

Social engineering relies on:
- Trust  
- Urgency  
- Authority  
- Curiosity  

Attackers trick individuals into revealing confidential information or performing unsafe actions.

---

## Common Techniques

- Phishing  
- Pretexting  
- Impersonation  
- Baiting  

---

## Example Scenario

An attacker sends an email pretending to be from IT support, asking the user to reset their password using a fake link.

The user enters their credentials, which are then stolen.

---

## Prevention

- Verify identities before sharing information  
- Be cautious with unexpected requests  
- Avoid clicking unknown links  
- Participate in security awareness training  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Social engineering targets human behavior  
- It is one of the most common attack methods  
- Awareness is the best defense  

---

## Notes

Understanding human psychology is crucial for preventing social engineering attacks.
