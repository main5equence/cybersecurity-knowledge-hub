# Phishing

## Overview

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where attackers attempt to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

It is typically carried out through emails, messages, or fake websites.

---

## How Phishing Works

1. The attacker sends a fraudulent message  
2. The message appears legitimate (e.g., bank, company)  
3. The user is asked to click a link or provide information  
4. The attacker captures the data  

---

## Common Signs

- Urgent or threatening language  
- Suspicious links  
- Unexpected attachments  
- Misspelled domains  

---

## Example

An email claims that your account has been compromised and asks you to log in immediately via a provided link.

The link leads to a fake login page.

---

## Prevention

- Check sender details carefully  
- Avoid clicking unknown links  
- Use multi-factor authentication  
- Report suspicious emails  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks  
- It relies on deception and urgency  
- Awareness significantly reduces risk  

---

## Notes

Phishing attacks are constantly evolving and becoming more sophisticated.
