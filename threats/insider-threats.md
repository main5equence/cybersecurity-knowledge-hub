# Insider Threats

## Overview

Insider threats originate from individuals within an organization who have authorized access to systems and data.

These threats can be intentional or accidental.

---

## Types of Insider Threats

- Malicious insiders (intentional harm)  
- Negligent insiders (carelessness)  
- Compromised insiders (account takeover)  

---

## Risks

- Data leaks  
- Unauthorized access  
- System sabotage  

---

## Example

An employee downloads sensitive company data and shares it externally without authorization.

---

## Prevention

- Access control and least privilege  
- Monitoring user activity  
- Employee training  
- Strong authentication  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Insider threats come from trusted users  
- They can be difficult to detect  
- Proper monitoring and controls are essential  

---

## Notes

Managing insider threats requires both technical and organizational measures.
