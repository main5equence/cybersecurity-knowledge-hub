## Overview

The CIA Triad is a fundamental model in cybersecurity that represents three core principles used to guide security policies and practices:

- **Confidentiality**
- **Integrity**
- **Availability**

These three elements form the foundation of information security and are essential for protecting systems and data.

---

## Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible only to authorized individuals.

### Examples:
- Encryption of sensitive data  
- Access control (passwords, authentication systems)  
- Data classification  

### Threats:
- Unauthorized access  
- Data breaches  
- Insider threats

## Integrity

Integrity refers to maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data over its entire lifecycle.

### Examples:
- Hashing and checksums  
- Data validation  
- Version control systems  

### Threats:
- Data tampering  
- Malware  
- Man-in-the-middle attacks

## Availability

Availability ensures that systems and data are accessible when needed.

### Examples:
- Backup systems  
- Redundancy  
- System maintenance  

### Threats:
- DDoS attacks  
- Hardware failures  
- System outages

## Balancing the Triad

In real-world scenarios, these three principles often conflict with each other. For example:
- Increasing security (confidentiality) may reduce usability (availability)
- Strong integrity controls may impact system performance

Effective cybersecurity requires balancing all three elements depending on the context.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity principles  
- It helps guide security decisions and system design  
- Every security measure typically supports one or more of these three areas  

---

## Notes

Understanding the CIA Triad is essential for analyzing risks, designing secure systems, and evaluating potential threats.
