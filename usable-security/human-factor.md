# Human Factor in Cybersecurity

## Overview

The human factor is one of the most critical elements in cybersecurity. Many security incidents are caused not by technical failures, but by human actions.

Understanding human behavior is essential for designing effective security systems.

---

## Why Humans Matter

People interact with systems daily and can:
- Make mistakes  
- Be manipulated  
- Bypass security controls  

---

## Common Human-Related Risks

- Weak passwords  
- Falling for phishing attacks  
- Misconfiguring systems  
- Sharing sensitive information  

---

## Social Engineering Connection

Attackers often exploit human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

### Examples:
- Phishing emails  
- Pretexting  
- Impersonation  

---

## Mitigation Strategies

- Security awareness training  
- Clear and simple policies  
- User-friendly security systems  
- Regular reminders and updates  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Humans are often the weakest link in security  
- Security systems must account for human behavior  
- Education and usability are key to reducing risk  

---

## Notes

Improving the human factor is essential for building effective and sustainable cybersecurity strategies.
