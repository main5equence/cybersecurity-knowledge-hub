## Risk Matrix

A risk matrix is a visual tool used to assess and prioritize risks based on two key factors:

- **Likelihood** – how probable the risk is  
- **Impact** – how severe the consequences would be  

It helps organizations quickly identify which risks require immediate attention and which can be monitored or accepted.

## Simplified Risk Levels

Risk levels are typically categorized as:

- Low Risk
Minor impact and low probability → acceptable or monitor

- Medium Risk
Moderate impact or likelihood → mitigation recommended

- High Risk
High impact and/or high likelihood → immediate action required

---

## How to Use a Risk Matrix
- Identify the risk
- Estimate its likelihood
- Estimate its impact
- Map it on the matrix
- Prioritize based on its position

---

## Example

A phishing attack targeting employees:

Likelihood: High
Impact: Medium

→ Result: Medium–High Risk, requiring mitigation (e.g. training, email filtering)

---

## Notes

Risk matrices are widely used in cybersecurity, risk management, and business decision-making.

They provide a simple and intuitive way to prioritize risks, especially when dealing with limited resources.
