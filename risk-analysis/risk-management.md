# Risk Management

## Overview

Risk management in cybersecurity is the process of identifying, analyzing, and mitigating risks that could impact systems, data, or operations.

It helps organizations make informed decisions about security investments and priorities.

---

## Risk Components

Risk is typically defined by three elements:

- **Threat** – a potential cause of harm  
- **Vulnerability** – a weakness that can be exploited  
- **Impact** – the potential damage  

---

## Risk Management Process

1. Identify assets and risks  
2. Assess vulnerabilities and threats  
3. Evaluate impact and likelihood  
4. Implement controls  
5. Monitor and review  

---

## Risk Assessment

Risk can be evaluated based on:
- Likelihood of occurrence  
- Potential impact  

---

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

- Risk avoidance  
- Risk reduction  
- Risk transfer (e.g., insurance)  
- Risk acceptance  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Risk management supports decision-making  
- Not all risks can be eliminated  
- Prioritization is essential  

---

## Notes

Effective risk management balances security, cost, and usability.
