# Threat Modeling

## Overview

Threat modeling is a structured approach to identifying, analyzing, and addressing potential threats in a system.

It helps organizations proactively design secure systems.

---

## Purpose

- Identify potential threats early  
- Understand attack vectors  
- Improve system design  

---

## Key Elements

- Assets (what needs protection)  
- Threats (what can go wrong)  
- Vulnerabilities (weak points)  
- Attack vectors (how an attack happens)  

---

## Common Models

- STRIDE (Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, Elevation of Privilege)  
- Attack trees  

---

## Process

1. Define the system  
2. Identify assets  
3. Identify threats  
4. Analyze vulnerabilities  
5. Define mitigations  

---

## Benefits

- Improves security awareness  
- Reduces design flaws  
- Helps prioritize risks  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Threat modeling is proactive  
- It focuses on understanding potential attacks  
- It supports secure system design  

---

## Notes

Threat modeling is widely used in secure software development and system architecture.
